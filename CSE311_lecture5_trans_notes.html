What is Ethics?

  • Study of morality and principles guiding right and wrong behavior.
  • Not necessarily equivalent to law or personal beliefs.

Major Categories of Ethics

  • Metaethics: Fundamental questions of morality (not the focus of this class).
  • Normative Ethics: Practical guides to make ethical decisions.
  • Applied Ethics: Application of ethical theories to specific fields, e.g., medical ethics, legal ethics, computer ethics (ACM Code).

Three Major Frameworks of Ethics

  • Virtue Ethics: Emphasizes adopting an ethical character. Examples include classical Greek and Roman ethics, Aristotle's Nicomachean Ethics, and various religious and cultural ethical systems. Read More
  • Deontological Ethics: Duty or rule-based ethical theories. Examples are legal systems, professional codes of ethics, and Immanuel Kant's categorical imperatives. Read More
  • Consequentialist Ethics: Ethical decisions based on the outcomes, aiming to maximize benefits and minimize harm. Utilitarianism by Bentham and Mill are key examples in this framework. Read More
